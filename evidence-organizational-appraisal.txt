### Data Source Reliability

#### Primary Data Sources
**Data Source 1:** BLS JOLTS (Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey)
- **Reliability Rating:** High
- **Data Collection Method:** Federal statistical survey with rigorous methodology
- **Update Frequency:** Monthly with quarterly summaries and annual reports
- **Historical Availability:** 20+ years of consistent data collection
- **Known Limitations:** Aggregate level data may not reflect local market variations

**Data Source 2:** BLS OES (Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics)
- **Reliability Rating:** High  
- **Data Collection Method:** Comprehensive occupational wage survey with near-universal coverage
- **Update Frequency:** Annual updates with detailed occupational breakdowns
- **Historical Availability:** Consistent methodology for 15+ years with trend analysis
- **Known Limitations:** May lag current market changes, doesn't capture total compensation

**Data Source 3:** Industry Cost Research and Benchmarking Studies
- **Reliability Rating:** Medium-High
- **Data Collection Method:** Synthesis of academic research, consulting studies, and industry reports
- **Update Frequency:** Various - compiled from studies spanning 2019-2024
- **Historical Availability:** Sufficient data for trend analysis and validation
- **Known Limitations:** Some studies from larger organizations requiring adaptation to small business context

#### Data Integration Assessment
**Cross-System Consistency:** Excellent - BLS data sources use consistent industry classifications and geographic boundaries
**Data Definition Consistency:** High - standardized federal statistical definitions across data sources
