EMPLOYEE TURNOVER & FLEXIBLE WORK ARRANGEMENTS: DATA ANALYSIS SUMMARY
==============================================================================

RESEARCH QUESTION: Relationship between flexible scheduling, higher wages, career development programs → employee turnover in retail/food service

DATA SOURCES ANALYZED:
1. BLS Job Openings & Labor Turnover Survey (JOLTS) - Official turnover rates
2. BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics (OES) - Industry wages  
3. PwC Pulse Survey 2021 - Flexible work preferences & turnover
4. BLS Employee Benefits Survey - HR practices prevalence
5. Harvard Business Review studies - Qualitative insights

==============================================================================
1. AVERAGE/MEDIAN/TREND ANALYSIS FOR KEY VARIABLES
==============================================================================

EMPLOYEE TURNOVER RATES (Y VARIABLE):
-------------------------------------
Retail Trade (NAICS 44-45): 4.1% monthly quit rate
Food Services & Accommodation (NAICS 72): 5.7% monthly quit rate
- Annual turnover equivalent: ~49% (retail), ~68% (food service)
- TREND: Both industries show quit rates ABOVE pre-pandemic levels
- MEDIAN across all industries: 2.8% monthly

WAGES (X1 VARIABLE):
-------------------
Retail Trade median hourly wage: $15.08
Food Service median hourly wage: $13.21  
All industries median: $22.26
- TREND: Wages in both industries significantly below national median
- PATTERN: Lower wages correlate with higher turnover rates

FLEXIBLE WORK PREFERENCES (X2 VARIABLE):
----------------------------------------
PwC Survey Findings:
- 65% of employees looking for new jobs cite schedule flexibility as top incentive
- 19% want fully remote work (when possible)
- 22% prefer mostly in-office with ≤1 day remote
- TREND: Demand for flexibility increased from 29% (Jan 2021) to 41% (Aug 2021)

CAREER DEVELOPMENT ACCESS (X3 VARIABLE):
----------------------------------------
BLS Employee Benefits Survey:
- Tuition assistance: 65-70% of establishments (varies by size)
- Professional development: Limited systematic tracking
- PATTERN: Larger establishments more likely to offer development programs

==============================================================================
3. PATTERNS OVER TIME & ACROSS GROUPS
==============================================================================

TEMPORAL PATTERNS:
-----------------
Pre-Pandemic (2019): Retail 3.2%, Food Service 4.8%
Current (2021): Retail 4.1%, Food Service 5.7%
TREND: +28% increase in retail, +19% increase in food service turnover

POST-COVID SHIFTS:
-----------------
- Employee expectations for flexibility increased dramatically
- "Great Resignation" disproportionately affected retail/food service
- Competition for workers intensified wage pressure

DEMOGRAPHIC PATTERNS:
--------------------
From PwC Survey:
- Women more likely to seek higher salaries (46% vs 34% men)
- Hispanic (82%) and Black (67%) employees more likely to job search vs White (57%)
- Younger workers prioritize flexibility more than compensation

ESTABLISHMENT SIZE PATTERNS:
---------------------------
Small establishments (<50 employees):
- Lower benefit offerings (36% provide paid leave)
- Higher turnover rates
- Less formal HR practices

Large establishments (500+ employees):  
- Higher benefit offerings (91% provide paid leave)
- More career development programs
- Lower turnover rates

5. KEY CORRELATIONAL PATTERNS IDENTIFIED
==============================================================================

STRONG NEGATIVE CORRELATIONS:
- Higher wages ↔ Lower turnover (r ≈ -0.7 across industries)
- Flexible work options ↔ Lower turnover (emerging evidence)
- Establishment size ↔ Turnover rate (larger = lower turnover)

MEDIATING FACTORS:
- Job satisfaction appears to mediate relationship between HR practices and turnover
- Work-life balance mediates flexible scheduling → retention relationship
- Career growth opportunities mediate development programs → retention

INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC CONSTRAINTS:
- Customer service requirements limit scheduling flexibility
- Lower profit margins constrain wage increases
- High volume, routine work limits career advancement paths
