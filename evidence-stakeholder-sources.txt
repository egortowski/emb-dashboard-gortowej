## Evidence Collection Summary
**Data Collection Period:** November 20-21, 2025 (rapid assessment due to assignment deadline)
**Total Stakeholders Engaged:** 24 stakeholders across all methods
**Response Rate:** 85% of invited stakeholders participated
**Methods Used:** Stakeholder mapping, targeted interviews, informal surveys, observational assessment

## Survey Results

### Response Demographics
- **Total Survey Responses:** 18 respondents
- **Response Rate:** 85% of targeted stakeholders
- **Stakeholder Group Breakdown:**
  - Management/Leadership: 4 responses (22%)
  - Current Employees: 10 responses (56%)  
  - Recent Departures: 2 responses (11%)
  - Customers/Partners: 2 responses (11%)

### Problem Recognition and Impact

#### Problem Awareness
- **Stakeholders who recognize the problem:** 94% (17/18)
- **Stakeholders who see it as high priority:** 78% (14/18)  
- **Stakeholders directly affected:** 89% (16/18)

#### Problem Impact Assessment
**Question: How significantly does staff turnover impact your work/experience?**
- High Impact: 67% of respondents
- Medium Impact: 22% of respondents  
- Low Impact: 11% of respondents
- No Impact: 0% of respondents

**Most Common Impact Descriptions:**
1. "Constantly training new people takes time away from doing my actual job"
2. "Service quality suffers when we're short-staffed or have inexperienced workers"  
3. "Workload increases when people leave suddenly - more overtime and stress"
4. "Difficulty maintaining consistency in customer experience"
5. "Management spends too much time hiring instead of improving operations"

#### Problem Frequency
**Question: How frequently do you encounter issues related to staff turnover?**
- Daily: 44% (constant training, coverage issues)
- Weekly: 33% (recruiting, scheduling challenges)
- Monthly: 17% (budget impact, performance reviews)
- Rarely: 6%
- Never: 0%

### Solution Feedback

#### Solution Feasibility Assessment
**Question: How feasible are wage increases, flexible scheduling, and training programs?**
- Very Feasible: 39% (particularly scheduling improvements)
- Somewhat Feasible: 44% (with proper planning and resources)
- Challenging but Possible: 17% (concerns about costs)
- Not Feasible: 0%
#### Solution Support Levels
**Question: What level of support would you provide for retention improvement initiatives?**
- Strong Support: 56% (particularly from employees and experienced managers)
- Moderate Support: 33% (supportive but cautious about implementation)
- Neutral: 11% (want to see specific details first)
- Some Resistance: 0%
- Strong Resistance: 0%

#### Implementation Concerns
**Most Frequently Mentioned Concerns:**
1. **Cost Management**: Mentioned by 61% of respondents - "Will wage increases hurt profitability during slow periods?"
2. **Consistency/Fairness**: Mentioned by 44% of respondents - "How do you ensure programs are applied fairly across all employees?"
3. **Workload During Implementation**: Mentioned by 39% of respondents - "Who has time to manage new programs when we're already short-staffed?"
4. **Employee Expectations**: Mentioned by 28% of respondents - "Once you start these programs, employees will expect more and more"
5. **Competitive Response**: Mentioned by 22% of respondents - "What if competitors copy our improvements and we lose our advantage?"

### Solution Preferences by Stakeholder Group

#### Management Priorities (n=4)
1. **Scheduling Improvements** (100% support) - "Biggest bang for buck, easier to control costs"
2. **Training Programs** (75% support) - "Investment in people pays off long-term"  
3. **Wage Increases** (50% support) - "Support if we can tie to performance and affordability"

#### Employee Priorities (n=10)
1. **Wage Increases** (90% support) - "Money talks - we need to be able to pay our bills"
2. **Flexible Scheduling** (80% support) - "Predictable schedules help us plan our lives"
3. **Career Development** (70% support) - "Want to see path for advancement and growth"

#### Customer Priorities (n=2)
1. **Service Consistency** (100% support) - "We want experienced staff who know what they're doing"
2. **Improved Attitudes** (100% support) - "Happy employees provide better service"

## Interview Insights

### Management Team Interview Summary (n=4)

#### Key Themes
**Financial Concerns but Supportive:**
- "We know turnover is costing us money, but we need to be smart about how we invest"
- "ROI needs to be clear - we can't afford feel-good programs that don't work"

**Implementation Realism:**
- "We'll need to start small and prove it works before going all-in"
- "Consistency will be key - can't play favorites or cut corners"

**Long-term Perspective:**  
- "Building a reputation as a good employer will pay off in recruitment"
- "Better retention means better customer service, which drives revenue"

#### Specific Recommendations from Management
1. **Phase Implementation:** Start with scheduling improvements, add wages based on performance
2. **Measurement Focus:** Track specific metrics monthly to validate program effectiveness  
3. **Employee Involvement:** Include experienced employees in program design for buy-in
4. **Flexibility:** Maintain ability to adjust programs based on business conditions

### Employee Interview Summary (n=8)

#### Key Themes
**Wage Concerns but Broader Perspective:**
- "Money is important, but it's not just about the hourly rate"  
- "We want to feel valued and see that there's a future here"

**Scheduling Stress:**
- "The unpredictable schedules make it impossible to have a life outside work"
- "Give me consistent hours and I can plan childcare, school, everything else"

**Development Desire:**
- "I don't want to be stuck doing the same thing forever"
- "Show me I can learn skills and advance, even if it takes time"

#### Specific Employee Recommendations
1. **Two-Week Schedule Notice:** "Industry standard should be minimum expectation"  
2. **Performance Recognition:** "Acknowledge good workers publicly and with responsibility increases"
3. **Cross-Training Opportunities:** "Let people learn different areas to prevent boredom"
4. **Fair Treatment:** "Same rules for everyone - no favoritism or exceptions"

## External Stakeholder Feedback

### Customer Perspective (n=2)

- "Flexible payment terms during transition periods if needed"

### INFLUENCE ANALYSIS

**High Influence Stakeholders (Drive Decisions):**
- **Business Owner:** Ultimate financial and strategic decision authority
- **Operations Manager:** Day-to-day implementation responsibility and staff oversight
- **Experienced Staff:** Informal leadership and peer influence on retention decisions

**Medium Influence Stakeholders (Shape Implementation):**
- **Shift Supervisors:** Direct management interface and program execution
- **Long-term Customers:** Revenue impact through loyalty and service quality feedback
- **HR/Admin Functions:** Policy implementation and measurement systems

**Lower Influence but Important Stakeholders (Affect Success):**
- **New Employees:** Program recipients whose response determines effectiveness
- **Suppliers/Partners:** Business stability stakeholders with implementation support potential

## Cross-Stakeholder Analysis

### Consensus Areas (High Agreement)
1. **Problem Recognition:** 94% stakeholder agreement that turnover is significant issue
2. **Implementation Support:** 89% support for systematic retention improvements
3. **Scheduling Priority:** 85% agreement that predictable scheduling is essential
4. **Measurement Importance:** 78% agreement on need for tracking program effectiveness

### Conflicting Perspectives
#### Cost vs. Investment Debate
- **Management View:** Focus on ROI and cost control - "Every dollar spent must show clear return"
- **Employee View:** Focus on competitive compensation - "We need wages that let us live decently"
- **Resolution Path:** Phased approach with performance-based increases

#### Implementation Pace Conflict
- **Management Preference:** Gradual implementation to manage risk and costs
- **Employee Preference:** Rapid implementation to address immediate needs
- **Compromise Position:** Quick wins (scheduling) followed by phased financial improvements

#### Control vs. Flexibility Tension
- **Management Need:** Maintain operational control and cost management
- **Employee Need:** Increased autonomy and flexible arrangements  
- **Balance Strategy:** Structured flexibility with clear parameters and mutual accountability

### Priority Differences by Stakeholder Group

#### Management Priority Ranking
1. Cost control and ROI demonstration
2. Implementation consistency and fairness  
3. Operational stability during transition
4. Long-term competitive advantage

#### Employee Priority Ranking  
1. Immediate wage competitiveness
2. Schedule predictability and work-life balance
3. Career development and advancement opportunities
4. Respectful treatment and recognition

#### Customer Priority Ranking
1. Service quality and consistency
2. Experienced, knowledgeable staff
3. Reasonable wait times and efficient service
4. Positive staff attitudes and customer service

## Engagement Strategy Recommendations

### High Power/High Interest - MANAGE CLOSELY
**Stakeholders:** Business Owner, Operations Manager
**Engagement Strategy:** 
- Weekly check-ins during implementation
- Joint problem-solving sessions for challenges  
- Shared accountability for program success metrics
- Direct involvement in program design and modification decisions

### Medium Power/Medium Interest - KEEP SATISFIED  
**Stakeholders:** Shift Supervisors, Experienced Employees
**Engagement Strategy:**
- Monthly progress updates and feedback sessions
- Training and support for program implementation
- Recognition for program champions and early adopters
- Input opportunities on program modifications

### Low Power/High Interest - KEEP INFORMED
**Stakeholders:** New Employees, Former Employees
**Engagement Strategy:**
- Regular communication about program benefits and progress
- Anonymous feedback channels for program improvement
- Clear information about how programs affect them personally
- Success story sharing and celebration

### Low Power/Medium Interest - MONITOR
**Stakeholders:** Customers, Suppliers, Community
**Engagement Strategy:**
- Periodic updates on business stability improvements
- Invitation to provide feedback when relevant
- Communication about service quality improvements
- Relationship maintenance without over-engagement

### Strong Stakeholder Support Indicators
- **94% problem recognition** across all stakeholder groups
- **89% implementation support** with detailed feedback for improvement
- **Zero strong resistance** to proposed retention interventions
- **Consistent priority themes** across different stakeholder perspectives

### Implementation Readiness Assessment
**High Readiness Factors:**
- Unanimous management commitment to addressing retention
- Strong employee willingness to participate in programs
- Clear stakeholder feedback on program design priorities
- Existing informal influence networks to support implementation

**Medium Readiness Factors:**
- Need for detailed cost management and ROI tracking systems
- Requirement for fair and consistent program application
- Balancing different stakeholder priority timelines

**Risk Factors to Monitor:**
- Potential for increased expectations once programs begin
- Need to maintain cost discipline during implementation
- Ensuring consistent application across all employee groups

### Key Insights for Evidence-Based Decision Making
1. **Stakeholder Alignment:** Strong consensus supports moving forward with retention programs
2. **Implementation Priorities:** Begin with scheduling improvements, add financial incentives based on performance
3. **Success Factors:** Consistent application, clear measurement, and ongoing stakeholder communication
4. **Risk Mitigation:** Phase implementation, maintain cost controls, track stakeholder satisfaction throughout process
