## STUDY SELECTION CRITERIA

### Inclusion Criteria:
✅ Peer-reviewed research published in academic journals
✅ Studies with quantifiable effect sizes (r, d, OR, β coefficients)
✅ Focus on retail, food service, or similar service industries  
✅ Interventions related to wages, scheduling, training, or management practices
✅ Sample sizes >100 participants for adequate statistical power

### Exclusion Criteria:
❌ Non-peer reviewed sources (trade publications, white papers)
❌ Studies without statistical effect sizes
❌ Research from unrelated industries (manufacturing, healthcare, etc.)
❌ Theoretical papers without empirical data
❌ Case studies without control groups

### Quality Indicators Sought:
- Published in high-impact management or psychology journals
- Studies with control groups or longitudinal designs
- Clear methodology and statistical reporting
- Replication studies or meta-analyses preferred

## Selection Criteria

### Inclusion Criteria
**Studies WILL be included if they:**
- Focus on employee turnover in retail or food service industries
- Include chains, franchises, or large corporations (50+ employees)
- Examine turnover causes, consequences, or reduction strategies
- Use rigorous quantitative or qualitative methodologies
- Published in peer-reviewed journals or reputable industry sources
- Provide actionable insights for management practices
- Include sufficient sample sizes for generalizability

### Exclusion Criteria  
**Studies will be EXCLUDED if they:**
- Focus solely on very small businesses (<10 employees)
- Examine industries outside retail/food service without transferable insights
- Use weak methodologies (poor sampling, no control groups, etc.)
- Are purely theoretical without empirical data
- Are older than 10 years unless seminal/foundational research
- Focus on unionized environments without broader applicability
- Are opinion pieces, blogs, or non-peer-reviewed sources

## Quality Assessment Approach
[How will you evaluate study quality?]

### Research Design Hierarchy
1. **Systematic Reviews/Meta-analyses** [Highest quality]
2. **Randomized Controlled Trials** [High quality]
3. **Quasi-experimental Studies** [Medium-high quality]
4. **Cohort Studies** [Medium quality]
5. **Cross-sectional Studies** [Medium-low quality]
6. **Case Studies** [Lower quality but valuable for context]

### Quality Criteria Checklist
**For each study, evaluate:**
- [✓] Clear research question addressing turnover in retail/food service
- [✓] Appropriate methodology (quantitative/qualitative standards met)
- [✓] Adequate sample size (>100 for quantitative, saturation for qualitative)
- [✓] Valid measurement tools (established turnover metrics, validated scales)
- [✓] Appropriate statistical analysis (significance testing, effect sizes reported)
- [✓] Clear reporting of results (tables, figures, confidence intervals)
- [✓] Discussion of practical implications for management
- [✓] Acknowledgment of study limitations
- [✓] Peer-reviewed publication in reputable journal
- [✓] Relevance to chain/corporate environments

**PRIORITY SCORING:**
- **High Priority (Score 8-10):** Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, large-scale studies
- **Medium Priority (Score 5-7):** Well-designed single studies with good samples
- **Low Priority (Score 3-4):** Smaller studies, single-company case studies
- **Exclude (Score 1-2):** Poor methodology, irrelevant context, insufficient data
