üìù Problem Definition: High Employee Turnover in Retail & Food Service

PICOC Framework
Population: Hourly front-line employees in retail and food service industry operations (cashiers, sales associates, food preparation staff, servers, and shift supervisors)
Intervention: Comprehensive retention strategy including onboarding programs, competitive compensation, flexible scheduling, clear career development pathways, peer recognition systems, manager training on employee engagement
Comparison: Current industry standard practices: minimal 1-2 day orientation, base wage compensation, strict scheduling, limited advancement opportunities, traditional management approaches
Outcomes: Primary: Reduce turnover from 75-100% to 40-60% within 24 months. Secondary: Decrease turnover-related costs by 35-45%
Context: Large retail chains and food service operations with corporate oversight, and set management structures in competitive labor markets across the United States
Problem Statement: Excessive employee turnover rates (75-100% annually) in the retail and food service industry creating operational costs, service inconsistency, and competitive disadvantage
